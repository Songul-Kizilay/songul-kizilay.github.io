I approach security work like an operator: map the surface, validate the signal, prove the impact, and explain it clearly. My lane is web application security, bug bounty, red team operations, and practical offensive validation.

The point is not to collect tools. The point is to turn Burp traffic, recon data, telemetry, and weak logic into defensible findings and practical action for the team on the other side of the report.

Positioning

Batman University Computer Engineering graduate. I read applications, APIs, and infrastructure as systems that can be mapped, stressed, misused, and explained under real-world pressure.

CEH Practical and the BGA Penetration Tester Training Program gave me structured lab discipline. Field work turned that discipline into instinct: validate manually, follow the attack path, and make every finding technically defensible.

On Bugcrowd, I reported ten different issues affecting real web applications and exposed services. The profile includes reflected XSS, CORS misconfiguration, open redirect, IDOR, access control problems, SSRF, origin IP disclosure, and cloud/config exposure patterns. That sharpened my ability to separate noise from exploitable behavior and to write findings that help remediation teams, not just screenshots.

Enforsec pushed my direction firmly toward red team work. Infinitum IT and the Turkish Ministry of Interior gave me earlier security operations context, but the center of gravity for me is offensive testing, exploit validation, and attack-path thinking.

At BITES Defense Aviation and Space Technologies, I worked around monitoring, security-oriented RPA workflows, and open-source research. At Deneyap Türkiye and CyberHero Academy, I also supported training, lab preparation, and mentoring. I do not only find problems. I also build scenarios, explain them clearly, and make the output usable.
