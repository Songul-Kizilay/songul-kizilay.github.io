About Me

Batman University Computer Engineering graduate. I approach applications, APIs, and infrastructure as systems that can be mapped, stressed, misused, and understood under real-world pressure.

CEH Practical and BGA Penetration Tester Training Program gave me the lab discipline; field experience turned that discipline into instinct. My workflow is built around manual validation, attack path thinking, and making each finding technically defensible.

On Bugcrowd, I reported ten different security issues affecting real web applications. That experience sharpened my ability to separate noise from exploitable behavior and to write findings that are useful for remediation teams, not just impressive for screenshots.

Enforsec gave me red team exposure. Infinitum IT and the Turkish Ministry of Interior gave me blue team and SOC visibility. Together, those environments taught me to understand both how an attack chain is built and how it is detected, monitored, and explained.

At BITES Defense Aviation and Space Technologies, I supported monitoring with Zabbix, contributed to security-related RPA workflows, and helped reduce risk through open-source research. At Deneyap Turkiye and CyberHero Academy, I also supported training, lab preparation, and mentoring. That means I do not just find problems. I also build scenarios, explain them clearly, and turn security knowledge into something people can actually use.
